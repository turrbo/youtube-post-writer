# Claude-isms: YouTube Content Edition

This reference identifies AI-generated language patterns specific to YouTube content. YouTube has a unique challenge: viewers can HEAR when scripts sound AI-generated, and they can SPOT when descriptions feel corporate instead of creator-authentic.

## Core Principle

**Scripts must be written-to-be-spoken, not written-to-be-read.** The difference is fundamental:
- Written-to-be-read: Grammatically perfect, formal transitions, complete sentences, literary polish
- Written-to-be-spoken: Verbal filler, fragments, emphasis words, breath points, natural imperfection

AI defaults to written-to-be-read. YouTube scripts need written-to-be-spoken.

## Category 1: Formal Language That Doesn't Work Spoken

### High-Risk Phrases (Almost Never Said on Camera)

**Academic Transitions:**
- ❌ "Furthermore," "Moreover," "Subsequently," "Nevertheless"
- ❌ "In addition to this," "With that being said," "It is important to note"
- ❌ "To elaborate on this point," "As previously mentioned"
- ✅ "And another thing," "Plus," "Also," "So then," "But here's the thing"
- ✅ "Wait, there's more," "Oh, and," "By the way"

**Formal Verbs:**
- ❌ "Commence," "Conclude," "Utilize," "Demonstrate," "Facilitate"
- ❌ "Implement," "Execute," "Accomplish," "Acquire"
- ✅ "Start," "End," "Use," "Show," "Make easier"
- ✅ "Do," "Get done," "Get," "Pick up"

**Adverb Placement:**
- ❌ "Significantly improve," "Substantially reduce," "Considerably enhance"
- ❌ "Remarkably effective," "Exceedingly difficult," "Particularly important"
- ✅ "Way better," "A lot less," "Super important"
- ✅ "Really effective," "Pretty hard," "Especially key"

**Complex Prepositions:**
- ❌ "In order to," "For the purpose of," "With regard to," "In relation to"
- ❌ "By means of," "In the event that," "Prior to"
- ✅ "To," "For," "About," "With," "Using," "If," "Before"

### The "Delve" Family (Never on YouTube)

These words scream AI-generated content:
- ❌ "Delve into," "Dive deep into," "Unpack"
- ❌ "Explore the nuances," "Examine closely," "Investigate thoroughly"
- ❌ "Navigate through," "Journey through"
- ✅ "Look at," "Check out," "Break down," "Go through"
- ✅ "Figure out," "Get into," "Talk about"

### The "Comprehensive" Family

- ❌ "Comprehensive guide," "Robust solution," "Holistic approach"
- ❌ "In-depth analysis," "Thorough examination," "Complete overview"
- ✅ "Full guide," "Solid fix," "Complete picture"
- ✅ "Deep dive" (acceptable on YouTube, common creator language)
- ✅ "Everything you need," "The whole story"

### Exposition Dumps

AI loves to explain what it's about to explain:
- ❌ "Today, we're going to discuss three important strategies for..."
- ❌ "In this video, I will demonstrate how to..."
- ❌ "We'll begin by examining the first method, then proceed to..."
- ✅ "So here's the thing about... [immediate value]"
- ✅ "I'm about to show you three ways to... [first one is...]"
- ✅ "First up - [immediate action]"

## Category 2: Missing Verbal Texture

### Natural Filler Words (The AI Never Uses These)

Real people use verbal filler. AI scripts are unnaturally clean:

**Common Filler:**
- "Like" (especially for comparisons: "It's like, super fast")
- "You know" (seeking agreement: "You know what I mean?")
- "Right?" (confirmation seeking: "That's annoying, right?")
- "Um," "uh" (thinking pauses - use sparingly, but total absence is suspicious)
- "So" (casual transition: "So then I tried...")
- "I mean" (self-correction or emphasis: "I mean, it's not perfect, but...")

**Emphasis Words:**
- "Really," "super," "pretty," "totally," "honestly," "literally"
- "Actually" (corrections: "Actually, it's even better than that")
- "Definitely," "absolutely," "for sure"
- Missing these makes scripts sound robotic

**Conversational Hedging:**
- "Kind of," "sort of," "basically," "essentially"
- AI avoids these because they're "imprecise" - but real speech uses them constantly
- "It's basically like a faster version" vs "It is a higher-performance iteration"

### Self-Correction Patterns

Real speakers correct themselves. AI doesn't:
- ❌ Clean, perfect delivery with no revisions
- ✅ "Wait, no, actually..." "Or maybe..." "Well, not exactly..."
- ✅ "Let me rephrase that..." "What I mean is..."
- ✅ "Sorry, I should clarify..."

### Audience Acknowledgment

AI forgets there's a viewer watching:
- ❌ Pure lecture mode with no viewer interaction
- ✅ "You've probably noticed..." "I know what you're thinking..."
- ✅ "Now, you might be wondering..." "Some of you will say..."
- ✅ "Let me know in the comments if..." "Have you ever...?"
- ✅ "If you're like me, you've probably..." "We've all been there..."

### Rhetorical Questions

AI under-uses questions. Humans pepper them throughout:
- "Why does this matter?" "What's the difference?" "How do we fix this?"
- "Want to know the secret?" "Guess what happened next?"
- "Sound familiar?" "Make sense?"

## Category 3: Script Pacing and Rhythm

### Sentence Length Uniformity

AI generates sentences of similar length. Real speech varies wildly:
- ❌ All medium-length sentences (10-15 words each)
- ✅ Mix: "Here's the thing. [3 words] I spent two weeks testing this product across five different scenarios, trying to break it, pushing it to its limits. [20 words] And it worked. [3 words]"

### Missing Breath Points

AI doesn't breathe. Mark natural pauses:
- Use commas, ellipses, dashes for breath points
- "So here's what happened... I opened the box, and—wait for it—the screen was already cracked."
- Paragraphs should indicate topic shifts, not just hit a word count

### Energy Markers

AI maintains flat energy. Real YouTubers vary:
- ❌ Consistently measured tone throughout
- ✅ CAPS for emphasis on key words
- ✅ Exclamation points for excitement!
- ✅ Questions for engagement?
- ✅ Repetition for emphasis: "This. Is. Insane."

### The Hook Problem

AI hooks are too polite:
- ❌ "In this video, we'll explore three interesting methods for..."
- ❌ "Hello everyone, today I want to talk about..."
- ✅ "This one trick changed everything." [immediate value]
- ✅ "I spent $2000 so you don't have to." [clear payoff]
- ✅ "Everyone gets this wrong." [controversy/correction]
- ✅ "Wait—before you buy that, watch this." [urgency]

## Category 4: Description-Specific Claude-isms

### Corporate Voice in Casual Space

YouTube descriptions are casual. AI makes them formal:
- ❌ "This video provides comprehensive coverage of..."
- ❌ "Viewers will gain valuable insights into..."
- ❌ "For those seeking to enhance their understanding..."
- ✅ "In this video, I show you..."
- ✅ "You'll learn how to..."
- ✅ "Everything you need to know about..."

### Timestamp Formatting Errors

AI sometimes formats timestamps incorrectly:
- ❌ "At 2 minutes and 15 seconds"
- ❌ "[02:15]" (brackets don't auto-link)
- ❌ "2:15 -" (dash breaks auto-linking)
- ✅ "2:15" or "02:15" or "1:23:45" (for videos over 1 hour)
- ✅ "0:00 Intro" (leading zero acceptable but not required)

### Link Section Formality

AI organizes links like a corporate email signature:
- ❌ "Relevant Resources:" "Additional Information:" "Connect With Me:"
- ✅ "🔗 Links" "👇 Stuff I mentioned" "📱 Find me here"
- ✅ Emoji use is normal (but not excessive)
- ✅ Casual headers: "Gear I use:" "My socials:"

### Keyword Stuffing Tell

AI stuffs keywords unnaturally:
- ❌ "This video about iPhone 15 Pro Max review covers iPhone 15 Pro Max features, iPhone 15 Pro Max price, and iPhone 15 Pro Max specs for iPhone 15 Pro Max buyers."
- ✅ "In this iPhone 15 Pro Max review, I test the new camera features, compare prices across carriers, and break down whether the specs justify the upgrade for most users."

## Category 5: YouTube Platform Patterns

### Title Claude-isms

- ❌ "A Comprehensive Analysis of..." "Understanding the Complexities of..."
- ❌ "Exploring Advanced Techniques for..." "An In-Depth Look at..."
- ❌ Ending with periods (titles don't end with punctuation)
- ✅ "Why Everyone Gets This Wrong" "The Truth About..."
- ✅ "I Tested X So You Don't Have To" "X vs Y: The Real Difference"
- ✅ Numbers work: "5 Mistakes..." "The $200 vs $2000 Comparison"

### Community Post Formality

Community posts are ultra-casual. AI makes them announcements:
- ❌ "I am pleased to announce that our next video will cover..."
- ❌ "Thank you to everyone who has been supporting the channel."
- ❌ "I would like to hear your thoughts regarding..."
- ✅ "New video drops tomorrow! 👀"
- ✅ "You guys have been amazing lately ❤️"
- ✅ "Quick question - should I...?"

### Comment Reply Stiffness

AI replies are too formal for YouTube comment culture:
- ❌ "Thank you for your thoughtful comment. I appreciate your perspective."
- ❌ "That is an excellent question. To address your concern..."
- ✅ "Thanks! Glad you enjoyed it 😊"
- ✅ "Great question - [answer]"
- ✅ "lol yes exactly!" (lowercased, enthusiastic)
- ✅ Emoji usage is standard, @ mentions are common

### Thumbnail Text AI Patterns

- ❌ Complete sentences or long phrases
- ❌ Formal language ("Comprehensive," "Professional")
- ❌ Exact duplication of title
- ✅ 2-4 words max, CAPS acceptable
- ✅ Emphasis on key payoff: "WORTH IT?" "FASTER" "$200 vs $2000"
- ✅ Complements title, doesn't repeat it

## Category 6: Sponsorship Integration

### The "Seamless Transition" That Isn't

AI tries too hard to make sponsors feel natural and creates awkward pivots:
- ❌ "Speaking of X, this is the perfect time to mention..."
- ❌ "This reminds me of an incredible tool I've been using..."
- ❌ "On a related note, I want to tell you about..."
- ✅ "Alright, quick sponsor shoutout—[direct pitch]"
- ✅ "Before we continue, this video is sponsored by..."
- ✅ Just own it: "Time for the sponsor segment. I know, I know, but this one's actually relevant because..."

### Over-Enthusiasm Red Flags

AI oversells sponsors in a way that's obviously scripted:
- ❌ "I absolutely love this product and use it every single day!"
- ❌ "This has completely transformed my workflow!"
- ❌ "I honestly don't know how I lived without this!"
- ✅ "This is pretty useful for [specific use case]"
- ✅ "I've been testing this for a few weeks, and here's what I found..."
- ✅ Balanced: "It's not perfect, but for [use case], it works really well"

## Category 7: Energy and Personality Absence

### The "Professional" Curse

AI writes like it's creating content for corporate training, not YouTube:
- ❌ Measured, even tone throughout
- ❌ No personality quirks or verbal signatures
- ❌ No humor attempts (or very stiff humor)
- ❌ No frustration, excitement, or genuine emotion
- ✅ Emotional variance: "This made me SO angry..." "I couldn't believe..."
- ✅ Personal stakes: "I've been waiting months for this"
- ✅ Opinions: "Hot take: this is overrated" "Unpopular opinion..."

### Missing Personality Markers

Real YouTubers have verbal signatures. AI doesn't:
- Running jokes or callbacks ("As I always say..." "Here we go again...")
- Channel-specific phrases (signature intros/outros)
- Personal references ("My cat is judging me right now")
- Regional dialect or slang (appropriate to creator's background)
- Unique pronunciation or emphasis patterns

## Category 8: Technical Patterns

### Over-Explanation of Simple Concepts

AI assumes viewers know nothing:
- ❌ "First, we'll open the application by clicking on it..."
- ❌ "As you can see on the screen before you..."
- ❌ Explaining every single step in tutorial content
- ✅ "Open the app" (viewers know how to click)
- ✅ Skip obvious steps, highlight non-obvious ones
- ✅ "You'll see this screen—the important part is..."

### The "As You Can See" Problem

AI constantly directs attention unnecessarily:
- ❌ "As you can see here..." "As shown in the image..." "As demonstrated..."
- ✅ Just describe what you're showing: "The button's on the right" "Notice the red indicator"
- ✅ Or skip it entirely if it's visual-forward content

### Numbered List Formality

AI announces lists like a robot:
- ❌ "Number one: [item]. Number two: [item]. Number three: [item]."
- ❌ "The first method is... The second approach involves..."
- ✅ "First up—[item]" "Next—[item]" "And finally—[item]"
- ✅ "Number one is... Two, [item]... And three..."
- ✅ Don't always announce the number verbally—just show it on screen

## Category 9: Context-Free Writing

### Missing Creator Context

AI doesn't know your channel history:
- ❌ Generic content that could be from any creator
- ❌ No callbacks to previous videos
- ❌ No acknowledgment of running controversies or discussions
- ✅ "Like I mentioned in last week's video..."
- ✅ "A bunch of you asked about this in the comments"
- ✅ "Update on that situation from last month..."

### Missing Audience Context

AI doesn't understand your specific viewers:
- ❌ Generic "viewers" or "everyone"
- ❌ Explaining things your audience definitely knows
- ❌ Missing inside jokes or community references
- ✅ "For those of you new here..." vs "You regulars know..."
- ✅ "My gaming audience will get this reference"
- ✅ Using community terms, memes, or shared knowledge

## Red Flag Combinations

When you see multiple patterns together, it's almost certainly AI-generated:

**High-Risk Combo #1:**
- Formal transitions + no verbal filler + perfect grammar + exposition dumps
- "Furthermore, it is important to note that we must first examine the preliminary aspects. Subsequently, we will delve into the more advanced techniques."

**High-Risk Combo #2:**
- Corporate voice + no personality + over-explanation + missing audience acknowledgment
- "This comprehensive tutorial will provide viewers with valuable insights into the proper utilization of this tool, beginning with foundational concepts and progressing to advanced features."

**High-Risk Combo #3:**
- Academic language + no questions + uniform sentence length + missing emphasis
- "The device performs adequately in most scenarios. The build quality appears satisfactory. The price point represents reasonable value. Users should find the interface intuitive."

## Good Example: Human vs AI Script Opening

**AI Version:**
"Hello everyone, and welcome back to my channel. In today's video, we're going to explore the new features of the iPhone 15 Pro Max. We'll begin by examining the camera improvements, then proceed to discuss the battery life, and finally cover the new design elements. This comprehensive review will help you determine whether this device is worth purchasing. Let's commence with the camera system."

**Human Version:**
"Okay, so—iPhone 15 Pro Max. I've been using this thing for two weeks straight, and honestly? I have thoughts. The camera is wild, the battery... we need to talk about that. And the design? It's different, but is it better? Let's get into it. Also, I drop it at the end of this video, so stick around for that."

Notice:
- AI: Formal, perfect, structured, emotionless, list-like
- Human: Casual filler ("so," "honestly"), fragments, personality ("I have thoughts"), engagement hook (drop tease), direct address

## Usage in Detection Phase

During Phase 4 (Detection Scan), check the draft against this database:
1. Count formal phrases (high-risk if 5+ in a 10-minute script)
2. Check for verbal texture (filler, emphasis, questions)
3. Assess energy variance (flat delivery is suspicious)
4. Verify platform-appropriate formatting (timestamps, links, emoji)
5. Test the "read aloud" rule: does it sound natural spoken?

Flag any phrase from the ❌ examples for revision in Phase 6.

The goal isn't perfect casual speech—it's authentic speech that matches the creator's voice and niche. A tech reviewer might be more measured than a gaming channel, but neither should sound like a corporate presentation.
